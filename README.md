# Los-Angeles-Crimes
The four-year period saw Los Angeles mirror national trends: a sharp spike in violent crime during the pandemic’s height, followed by a gradual stabilization. While 2023 brought a welcomed double-digit decrease in homicides, it also saw a rise in specific property crimes, particularly retail theft and motor vehicle theft.
# 🚔 Los Angeles Crime Analysis (2020–2023)

A data-driven exploration of crime patterns in Los Angeles using Python and public safety records from 2020 to 2023. This project cleans, processes, and analyzes crime data to uncover trends in victim demographics, crime types, and high-incidence areas.

---

## 📊 Project Overview
This analysis utilizes LAPD crime data to identify shifting safety landscapes over a four-year period. Key focuses include:
* **Cleaning & Preparation**: Handling missing values (e.g., imputing victim demographics and dropping incomplete weapon data).
* **Temporal Analysis**: Extracting time-based features like occurrence years and months to track crime surges.
* **Hotspot Identification**: Pinpointing high-crime bureaus and the most frequent types of offenses.

## 🚀 Features
* 🧹 **Data Sanitization**: Automatically handles missing entries and converts date strings to datetime objects.
* 📈 **Trend Visualization**: Analyzes crime fluctuations from 2020 through the end of 2023.
* 📍 **Geospatial Insights**: Identifies top crime areas such as **Central**, **Southwest**, and **77th Street**.
* 👤 **Demographic Profiling**: Explores victim age, sex, and descent patterns.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack
* **Python** 🐍
* **Pandas** (Data Manipulation) 🐼
* **Matplotlib & Seaborn** (Visualization) 📉
* **Jupyter Notebook** (Interactive Analysis) 📓

## 🔍 Key Findings & Recommendations
* **Recent Surges**: The analysis highlights a dramatic increase in reported crimes during 2022 and 2023, requiring further socio-economic investigation.
* **Top Offenses**: Vehicle burglaries are prevalent in the **Central** area, while **Southwest** and **77th Street** see higher rates of identity theft and assault.
* **Strategic Policing**: Recommendations include optimizing patrol schedules based on temporal patterns and tailoring prevention programs to specific area hotspots.
